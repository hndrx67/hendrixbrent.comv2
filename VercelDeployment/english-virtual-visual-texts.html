Understanding Virtual/Visual Texts

Topic: Understanding Virtual/Visual Texts

Virtual texts are digital texts like websites, blogs, e-books, and social media posts. They can include links, images, videos, and interactive parts you can click or tap.

Learning Outcomes

  1. Identify different types of virtual texts and their purposes.
  2. Describe features of virtual texts (links, images, videos).
  3. Explain main idea and supporting details in a virtual text.

Media to Use

  1. Interactive websites: National Geographic Kids, Scholastic News Online, Time for Kids.
  2. Blog examples: Kid-friendly travel blogs, science experiment blogs.
  3. E‑book samples: Epic Books, Sora.
  4. Multimedia examples: Articles with videos and infographics.
  5. Virtual text anatomy: Posters showing parts like title, menu, links, media.
  6. Worksheets: Identification and graphic organizers.

Activities

Practice identifying types and features of virtual texts.

1) Identify the Virtual Text Type

“A website post with pictures and a video explaining how rainbows form.” This is a …

2) Match the Feature to Its Name

A word you click to open a new page
A short line of text under an image
A list of buttons to navigate
Menu
Link
Caption

3) Main Idea vs. Details

Read this short blog snippet: “I visited the science museum. My favorite part was the space hall where I watched a rocket video. I also read facts about planets.” The main idea is …

Quick Tests

Q1) Which is a virtual text?

Q2) What does a caption do?

Q3) A menu helps you …
